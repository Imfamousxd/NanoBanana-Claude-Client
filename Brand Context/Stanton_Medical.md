# Stanton Medical Supply

Clinical, premium-medical, sterile **injection-prep supply** brand (syringes,
bacteriostatic water, alcohol prep pads, prep kits). Reads institutional and
clinical-premium — NOT a hazard/warning label. Website string used on labels:
`STANTON-MEDICAL-SUPPLY.COM`.

This document is fully standalone — everything needed to produce on-brand Stanton
content is written out below. Do not invent colors, copy, or claims beyond it.

---

## 1. Brand identity

- **Surface:** matte-WHITE dominant with abundant white space. White space is the
  brand's defining trait.
- **Palette (ONLY these):**
  - Deep medical **navy `#0F1C34`** — anchor / primary text
  - Chip navy `#1D3253`
  - ONE disciplined **clinical-teal accent `#1A9AA6` / `#36B8C2`** — used sparingly
  - Steel `#677889` / `#4E5D6C` — secondary text/specs
  - Navy-100 `#E2E8F0` — hairlines
  - **NO red, NO biohazard motifs, NO rainbow** — institutional clinical-premium only.
- **Typography (strict two-family):**
  - **Source Serif 4** — display, headings, the "Stanton" wordmark
  - **Libre Franklin** — everything else; uppercase eyebrows at ~0.14em tracking
- **Logo:** the navy **double-ring SEAL with a white medical cross + thin teal inner
  ring** (`Stanton Assets/stanton-seal.png`, generated by `make-stanton-seal.py`).
  Lockup = seal + wordmark "Stanton" with "MEDICAL SUPPLY" to its right, anchored
  **TOP-LEFT** of layouts.
- **The one color exception:** U-100 insulin syringes in hero renders carry realistic
  **ORANGE needle caps** (user's call — changed from teal). Everything else on the
  label stays navy/teal/steel/white.

## 5. Generation playbook

- **Model: gpt-image-2, `/v1/images/edits` endpoint**, with the seal passed as
  reference `image[1]` so the mark renders correctly. Kit label size: 1024×1536
  (5:6 portrait). Outputs into the `Stanton Assets/...` subfolders with timestamped
  names.
- **Generate 2–3 candidates** per subjective decision and let the user pick
  (the bac-water label was picked from candidates as "c1").
- **Prompt language that matters:**
  - Name the seal reference's role explicitly ("use the seal from reference image 1,
    top-left, with wordmark 'Stanton' + 'MEDICAL SUPPLY' to its right").
  - Spell every locked string exactly: "Stanton Peptide Prep Kit",
    "STANTON-MEDICAL-SUPPLY.COM", "30 mL Multiple-Dose Vial", "BACTERIOSTATIC
    WATER", "0.9% Benzyl Alcohol Added", "Premium Lab-Grade Quality", "For Research
    Use Only" — plus a negative: "no other text, no misspellings".
  - Keep the hero "small, compact, premium render of generic unbranded supplies";
    call out "realistic orange needle caps on the insulin syringes".
  - Palette guardrails in-prompt: white-dominant, deep navy #0F1C34 text, single
    teal accent, steel secondary, thin hairlines; "no red, no biohazard symbols".
- **Wide-label technique (the 2.24:1 dieline problem):** gpt-image-2 cannot output
  2.24:1 (its widest is 1536×1024 = 1.5:1). Locked workaround: generate 1536×1024
  with the design as a wide-SHORT **LETTERBOX** — all content in a central band with
  plain-white ~16% trim margins top AND bottom — then crop to the exact dieline with
  `sips -c 685 1536 <file>` → 1536×685. Verify the crop against the PDF dims.
- **Change-one-thing edits:** to alter a single element on an approved label (e.g.
  10mL → 30mL), pass the approved image as the first `image[]` and instruct
  "reproduce EXACTLY except <the one change>" (this is how the 30mL final was made).
- **Iterate by prompt, never composite** generated parts together; re-generate with
  better prompts + references.
- After generating, open results for review: `open -a Preview "<path>"`.
